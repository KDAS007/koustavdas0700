PhD Student in Computer Science · IIT Gandhinagar

I am a PhD student at IIT Gandhinagar, advised by Prof. Manoj Gupta. My research focuses on fault-tolerant distance oracles and approximate shortest path structures in graphs. I completed my M.Sc. at Ramakrishna Mission Vivekananda Educational and Research Institute and my B.Sc. at Ramakrishna Mission Vidyamandira.

I enjoy working on problems in combinatorics, complexity, and graph theory. I genuinely love conversations with other scholars—please feel free to reach out anytime.

Research Experience

Fault-Tolerant Distance Oracles · IIT Gandhinagar 2024 – present
Designing approximate single-source distance oracles resilient to dual edge failures. Achieved sub-quadratic space with (1+ε)-approximate query answers. Generalising towards f-fault structures. Work accepted at ESA 2026.
Connected Vertex Cover on AT-Free Graphs · RKMVERI 2022 – 2023
Devised a 1.5-approximation algorithm for connected vertex cover on AT-free graphs and investigated the price of connectivity on 2-connected AT-free graphs.
Travelling Salesman Problem · Carleton University (Remote) Summer 2022
Studied a genetic local search algorithm for symmetric TSP under Prof. Anil Maheshwari. Surveyed approximation techniques for NP-hard combinatorial optimisation.
Vaccine Supply Chain Optimisation · RKM Vidyamandira 2020 – 2021
Implemented Kruskal-based clustering for optimal-cost spanning structures in a vaccine supply management model (B.Sc. project, advised by Prof. Arindam Sarkar).
